Our Constitution

Article 1: Name 

The name of the organization shall be RED (Reforming Education on Drugs) Club, hereinafter referred to as RED. 

Article 2: Mission 

RED aims to provide in-class educational sessions regarding drug abuse prevention. Through the means of interactive scientific demonstrations and presentations, we will establish a foundational understanding of the biological mechanisms involved in drug addiction and the social implications involved. 

Article 3: Membership 

Membership will be open to all students at the University of Calgary, and to other members of the community. A valid membership will be recognized for the term September to August. The club will have a minimum of 20 members at all times to remain in operation, 2/3 of whom shall be active members of the Students’ Union. The club will collect a minimum $5.00 membership fee per individual member each year. As a member, an individual is entitled to have their voice heard by all other members.

Benefits and Rights: Furthermore, by joining RED, members are able to directly contribute to the well-being and education of younger students in their own communities. Members, also have prime opportunities to practice and perfect their presentation and communication skills, develop researching skills, and enhance their ability to communicate complex ideas in simpler terms. Members can attend meetings, participate in club events, run for executive positions, and all such activities. Executive Members may also participate in voting on decisions and planning of events.

Termination of Membership: Membership may be revoked if a member resigns or is asked to resign via an impeachment (Article 6). Executive members wishing to resign must provide their resignation in writing to all other members of the executive two weeks prior to their intended date of resignation.

Article 4: Students’ Union 

RED acknowledges and will abide by the Students’ Union Bylaws and Policies (unless otherwise approved by the Club Committee). We are aware the RED will cease to be a Students’ Union club should a Policy or Bylaw be violated without Club Committee ratification.

Article 5: Office and Duties

1. Co-President

  • Coordinate all operations of club and ensure they abide by Students’ Union Policies and By­laws
  • Serve as a co­-signing authorities for club financial transactions.
  • Work with other executive members to coordinate volunteering opportunities and partnerships with Elementary and Junior-High Schools.
  • Seek out and maintain mutually beneficial partnerships with on campus services, businesses and organizations, as well as after school service providers in the city.
  • Organize fundraising opportunities and events

2. Vice President External

  • Facilitate the communication between Elementary and Junior-High Schools with RED
  • Coordinate presentation dates, communicate club objectives
  • Ensure that the presentations meet the criteria/requirements for the school(s)
  • Oversee the use of the club’s logo

3. Vice President Internal

  • Communicate club meeting schedules, promotions, and other such events with club members
  • Track volunteer hours contributed by club members
  • Track meeting minutes and create meeting agendas
  • Keep an up-to-date list of active members

4. Treasurer

  • Responsible for all club financial matters, including acting as a co-signing authority for club financial transactions.  
  • Coordinate club purchases and funding requests. 
  • Submit the Annual Report to the Students’ Union
  • Maintain financial records

Article 6: Executive Terms of Office

The Co-President positions are appointed, the remaining executive positions are democratically determined. The term of office shall commence on May 1st and end on April 30th of the following school year. Both executive and general members must provide 2-weeks resignation notice. Resigning executive members are responsible for the transitioning of a new executive member, this new member will be appointed by the co-presidents and remain in office for the remainder of the term. 

Any member may be impeached for the following:

  • Mismanagement of finances
  • Failure to maintain responsibilities
  • Criminal/illicit behaviour (consistent)

Prior to official impeachment, two executive meetings must be held, the first including the executive in question, the second including only the other executives. The final decision to impeach must be unanimously reached, where the subject in question is not present. 

If an executive member is not present, his/her duties will be split by the other executive members.

Article 7: Executive Elections

A. The election of a new club Executive must take place no later than March 31st of each year to ensure a smooth transition from old Executive to new Executive.
B. Prior to the election, the Executive shall appoint a Chief Returning Officer (CRO) for the upcoming election. The CRO must be a person who is not running in the upcoming election. 
C. The CRO shall not vote in the upcoming election. 
D. The CRO shall be responsible for all duties associated with the upcoming election and shall provide proper notice to all club members of the upcoming election.
E. Voting shall take place online, all active club members are permitted to vote
F. Appealing Election Results: The individual behind this movement must present their reasons of appeal before the newly elected and past executive members, as well as the CRO. This committee of individuals will then vote on whether to disregard the appeal, re-vote for the specified position(s), or host another election entirely, depending on the reasons and evidence presented.
G. Proxy votes are allowed, under the following conditions: The member representing the absent member must have the decision (regarding the subject) signed and written by the absentee. 

Article 8: Transition of the Executive

For the month of April (beginning to end), the sitting executives will be focused on teaching the newly elected executives on club policies and procedures. The new executives will also be presented with club HUBS as well as custodianship of club records, materials, and bank/financial records. The outgoing executive is responsible for all year-end activities including the submission of the Annual Report to the SU, locker clean-out, and preparing of financial statements

Article 9: Meetings 

The President may call an Executive meeting at any time, on at least 24 hours notice. There shall be at least one Executive meeting and/or General Meeting per month during the academic year (September to April). Presence of 2/3 of Executive members shall constitute quorum. It is the executive duty of both the co-presidents to chair all meetings; however, this can be done individually if one of the co-presidents is not present. In a scenario in which both Co-presidents are unable to attend, any other executive member is expected to take-over. It is the role of Vice President Internal to record meeting minutes, notify club members of meetings and meeting agendas, and to make these records and agendas available to all members. 

Article 10: Amendments

This constitution shall comprise the operating basis of RED. All amendments to this document must be ratified by a two­thirds vote of the club membership. A copy of the amended constitution must be submitted to the Students’ Union within two weeks of ratification.
